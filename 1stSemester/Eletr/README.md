# Eletr — Electronics

## Overview

Electronics introduces the fundamental principles of electrical circuits and electronic components.  
The course focuses on understanding how current, voltage, and passive/active components interact within a circuit.

It provides the physical foundation necessary to understand how digital systems are implemented in real hardware.

## Topics Covered

- Basic electrical quantities (voltage, current, resistance, power)  
- Ohm’s Law and Kirchhoff’s Laws  
- Series and parallel circuits  
- Capacitors and inductors  
- Diodes and basic semiconductor devices  
- Introduction to transistors (if applicable)  
- Basic circuit analysis techniques  

## Learning Objectives

By the end of the course, the student should be able to:

- Analyze simple electrical circuits  
- Apply Kirchhoff’s laws to solve circuit problems  
- Understand the behavior of basic electronic components  
- Interpret circuit schematics  

## Relevance to the Degree

Electronics complements Digital Systems Logic by explaining the physical layer beneath digital circuits.  
It is particularly relevant for areas such as:

- Embedded systems  
- Hardware design  
- Signal processing  
- Low-level system engineering  

A solid understanding of circuit fundamentals is essential for anyone working close to hardware.
